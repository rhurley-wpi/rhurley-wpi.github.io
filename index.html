About Me

I am a second-year PhD student in Civil Engineering at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, advised by Professor Shichao Liu. My research bridges environmental engineering and public health, with a focus on indoor air quality, wildfire smoke exposure, occupant health and behavior. I am eager to learn more about community-engaged research practices, developing smoke or natural disaster readiness plans, and am looking to expand the interdisciplinary nature of my work.

My current projects examine re-emission of wildfire-sourced pollutants from building materials. Previously, I have investigated the impacts of elevated carbon dioxide exposure due to mask-wearing on student cognition during the COVID-19 pandemic. As an undergraduate student at Lafayette College, my research focused on predicting flash flooding behaviors in the mid Atlantic region.

More About My Work

As the student representative to the International Society for Indoor Air Quality and Climate (ISIAQ), I support the next generation of indoor air researchers by coordinating student mentoring, networking, and skill-building workshops. I also lead the 2025 Autumn School in India, planning a two-day event for students prior to the Healthy Buildings Asia conference.

At WPI, my work bridges environmental engineering and public health through community-engaged research. I am studying the re-emission of wildfire-sourced pollutants from building materials using advanced analytical techniques. I am also interested in how humans perceive and respond to indoor smoke exposure and what behaviors improve resilience.

Previously, I worked with San Jose State University on home ignition zone evaluations in California, collecting data and using drone imagery to support wildfire preparedness efforts.
